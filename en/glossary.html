Glossary of Key Terms

Knowledge System
A structured set of patterns, processes, and relations that generate, maintain, and transform knowledge. Knowledge systems can be biological, cultural, technological, or artificial.
Transition Sequence
The six‑phase cycle through which knowledge systems reorganize themselves: stabilization, tension accumulation, breakdown, exploratory expansion, re‑consolidation, and recursive embedding.
Stabilization
A phase in which structures become reproducible and resistant to change. Stabilization provides functional continuity but also generates tensions over time.
Breakdown
The collapse or destabilization of previously stable structures once accumulated tensions exceed a threshold. Breakdown opens the system to reorganization and variation.
Exploratory Expansion
A generative phase following breakdown in which the system produces a wide range of structural or conceptual variations. Most variants remain unstable, but some form the basis for new organizational patterns.
Re‑Consolidation
The process through which successful variants are stabilized and integrated into the system, forming a new structural baseline for future cycles.
Recursive Embedding
The mechanism by which stabilized structures from one cycle become components of a higher‑order cycle. Recursive embedding enables long‑term evolutionary development and increasing complexity.
