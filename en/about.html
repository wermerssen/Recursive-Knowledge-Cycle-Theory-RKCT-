About RKCT

The Recursive Knowledge Cycle Theory (RKCT) is a general framework for understanding how knowledge systems evolve. It describes how structures stabilize, accumulate tensions, break down, explore new configurations, and re‑consolidate at higher levels of organization.

Purpose of the Theory

RKCT aims to provide a unified model of structural change across biological, cultural, technological, and artificial systems.

Core Components

  • The 10 Axioms: Fundamental principles describing how structures emerge, vary, stabilize, and recursively generate new forms.
  • The Transition Sequence: A six‑phase model outlining how systems move from stabilization to breakdown, exploration, and re‑consolidation.
  • Recursive Embedding: The mechanism through which each completed cycle becomes part of a higher‑order structure.

Current Version

The current stable version of the model is RKCT 4.4.
