Glossary of Key Terms (RKCT 4.4)

This glossary defines the central concepts of the Recursive Knowledge Cycle Theory (RKCT 4.4). It provides the conceptual vocabulary required to understand the six‑phase transition sequence and its recursive embedding.

Knowledge System
A structured set of processes, relations, and constraints that generate, stabilize, transform, and reproduce knowledge over time. Knowledge systems may be biological, cultural, technological, or artificial.
Transition Sequence
The six‑phase sequence through which knowledge systems reorganize themselves: Stabilization → Tension Accumulation → Breakdown → Exploratory Expansion → Re‑Consolidation → Recursive Embedding.
Stabilization
A phase in which a system maintains a coherent and reproducible structure. Patterns are reinforced through repetition, feedback, and functional integration. Variability is low.
Tension Accumulation
Internal or external pressures increase as environmental conditions shift, inconsistencies grow, or resources become scarce. The stabilized structure becomes increasingly strained.
Breakdown
When accumulated tensions exceed the system’s capacity for stabilization, structural coherence collapses. Constraints weaken, variability increases, and the system becomes unstable.
Exploratory Expansion
The system generates a wide range of structural or conceptual variants. Most remain unstable, but some open new developmental pathways and potential stabilization opportunities.
Re‑Consolidation
Successful variants are stabilized and integrated into a new structural baseline. The system regains coherence at a higher level of organization. New constraints emerge that enable reliable reproduction of the new structure.
Recursive Embedding
The newly consolidated structure becomes embedded within a broader recursive cycle. Each completed transition forms the foundation for subsequent cycles, enabling long‑term evolutionary development.
Variant
A structural or conceptual deviation generated during exploratory expansion. Variants may be transient, unstable, or foundational for future consolidation.
Constraint
A stabilizing rule, boundary, or structural condition that shapes system behavior. Constraints weaken during breakdown and re‑emerge during re‑consolidation.
Structural Tension
The mismatch between a stabilized structure and a changing environment. Tension is the primary driver of transitions.
Recursive Cycle
A sequence in which each completed transition becomes the basis for the next. RKCT 4.4 models knowledge evolution as a potentially unbounded recursive process.
