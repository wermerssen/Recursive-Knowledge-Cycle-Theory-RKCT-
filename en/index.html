Recursive Knowledge Cycle Theory (RKCT)

The Recursive Knowledge Cycle Theory (RKCT) provides a unified framework for understanding how knowledge systems evolve across biological, cultural, technological, and artificial domains. It describes how structures stabilize, accumulate tensions, break down, explore new configurations, and eventually re‑consolidate into higher‑order forms. RKCT integrates these processes into a single recursive cycle that explains long‑term development and increasing complexity.

This English section contains the full model description, background information, glossary of key concepts, and a detailed history of how the theory developed across its major versions.
