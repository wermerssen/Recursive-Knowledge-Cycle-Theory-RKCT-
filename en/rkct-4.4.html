RKCT 4.4 – Core Model of the Transition Sequence

Version 4.4 of the Recursive Knowledge Cycle Theory (RKCT) describes a generalized transition sequence through which knowledge systems reorganize themselves and generate new levels of structure. The model identifies recurring phases that appear across biological, cultural, and technological evolution.

1. Initial Stabilization

A system maintains a stable configuration that allows reliable reproduction of its current knowledge structures. Variability is low, and the system optimizes within known boundaries.

2. Accumulation of Tensions

Internal or external pressures accumulate. The existing structure becomes insufficient to handle increasing complexity, environmental change, or internal contradictions.

3. Structural Breakdown

The previous organizational form loses coherence. Constraints weaken, variability increases, and the system enters a phase of instability.

4. Exploratory Expansion

The system generates a wide range of new configurations. Novel combinations, mutations, or conceptual variations emerge. Most are unstable, but some provide new pathways.

5. Re‑Consolidation

A new stable configuration forms. It integrates successful variations and establishes new constraints, enabling reproduction at a higher level of organization.

6. Recursive Embedding

The new structure becomes the basis for further cycles. The system can now generate, store, and transform knowledge at a higher level of abstraction.
