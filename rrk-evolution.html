Evolution of the RKCT Model

This page documents the development of the Recursive Knowledge Cycle Theory across its major conceptual versions. Each iteration refined the structure of the transition sequence and clarified the relationships between stabilization, breakdown, exploration, and re‑consolidation.

Version History Diagram

The following diagram summarizes the evolution of the RKCT model from early conceptual sketches to the current version 4.4. It visualizes how the transition sequence became more formalized and how recursive embedding was integrated as a core structural element.

Conceptual Milestones

  • 1.x → 2.x: Identification of destabilization as a structural phase.
  • 2.x → 3.x: Introduction of exploratory expansion as a distinct process.
  • 3.x → 4.x: Formalization of recursive embedding and higher‑order cycles.
  • 4.4: Current stable version with clarified phase boundaries.

Purpose of the Evolution Page

The evolution page serves as a transparent record of how the theory matured. It highlights the iterative refinement process and provides context for the current formulation of RKCT 4.4.
