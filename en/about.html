About RKCT – Background & Motivation

The Recursive Knowledge Cycle Theory (RKCT) emerged from the attempt to describe how knowledge systems reorganize themselves across different domains. Instead of focusing on specific mechanisms in biology, culture, or technology, RKCT identifies a shared structural pattern underlying transitions in all three.

Why RKCT?

Many theories describe evolution, learning, or innovation within a single domain. RKCT aims to provide a unified perspective by focusing on the transition sequence itself — the recurring phases through which systems destabilize, explore, and re‑stabilize at a higher level.

Scope of the Theory

RKCT does not attempt to replace domain‑specific models. Instead, it offers a meta‑level framework that helps compare processes across:

  • biological evolution
  • cultural and social development
  • technological innovation
  • knowledge‑based artificial systems

Motivation

The motivation behind RKCT is to understand why transitions often follow similar structural patterns, even when the underlying mechanisms differ. By identifying these patterns, RKCT provides a tool for analyzing large‑scale transformations and the emergence of new organizational levels.
