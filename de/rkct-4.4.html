RKCT 4.4 – Core Model of the Transition Sequence

Version 4.4 presents the most refined formulation of the Recursive Knowledge Cycle Theory. It describes a generalized transition sequence through which knowledge systems reorganize themselves, resolve accumulated tensions, and generate new levels of structure. The model consists of six phases that together form a recursive cycle.

1. Initial Stabilization

In the stabilization phase, a system maintains a coherent and reproducible structure. Patterns are reinforced through repetition, feedback, and functional integration.

2. Accumulation of Tensions

As conditions change, the stabilized structure becomes increasingly strained. Tensions arise from environmental shifts, internal inconsistencies, or resource constraints.

3. Structural Breakdown

When accumulated tensions exceed the system’s capacity for stabilization, breakdown occurs. The existing structure loses coherence and becomes unstable.

4. Exploratory Expansion

Following breakdown, the system generates a wide range of structural or conceptual variants. Most remain unstable, but some introduce new pathways for development.

5. Re‑Consolidation

Successful variants are stabilized and integrated into a new structural baseline. The system regains coherence at a higher level of organization.

6. Recursive Embedding

The newly consolidated structure becomes embedded within a broader recursive cycle. Each iteration builds upon the previous one, enabling long‑term evolutionary development.
