Glossary of Key Terms

This glossary provides concise definitions of central concepts used in the Recursive Knowledge Cycle Theory (RKCT). The terms are formulated in a domain‑neutral way to support cross‑disciplinary comparison.

Knowledge System
Any system capable of generating, storing, or transforming information in a structured manner. Includes biological, cultural, and technological systems.
Transition Sequence
The recurring pattern of destabilization, exploration, and re‑stabilization through which knowledge systems reorganize themselves.
Stabilization
A phase in which a system maintains a coherent structure that supports reliable reproduction of its knowledge.
Tension Accumulation
The buildup of internal or external pressures that challenge the existing structure of a system.
Breakdown
The loss of structural coherence that precedes exploratory expansion.
Exploratory Expansion
A phase of increased variability in which new configurations, combinations, or conceptual variations emerge.
Re‑Consolidation
The formation of a new stable configuration that integrates successful variations.
Recursive Embedding
The process by which a newly stabilized structure becomes the foundation for further cycles of knowledge generation.
