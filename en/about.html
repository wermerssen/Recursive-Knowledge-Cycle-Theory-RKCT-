About RKCT

The Recursive Knowledge Cycle Theory (RKCT) is a general framework for understanding how knowledge systems evolve. It describes how structures stabilize, accumulate tensions, break down, explore new configurations, and re‑consolidate at higher levels of organization. RKCT is not tied to a specific domain; it applies to biological evolution, cultural development, scientific progress, technological innovation, and artificial knowledge systems.

Purpose of the Theory

RKCT aims to provide a unified model of structural change. Instead of treating stability and transformation as opposing forces, the theory integrates them into a single recursive cycle. Each cycle produces new structures that become the foundation for the next iteration. This makes RKCT a powerful tool for analyzing long‑term development across different types of systems.

Core Components

The theory is built on three foundational elements:

  • The 10 Axioms: Fundamental principles describing how structures emerge, vary, stabilize, and recursively generate new forms.
  • The Transition Sequence: A six‑phase model outlining how systems move from stabilization to breakdown, exploration, and re‑consolidation.
  • Recursive Embedding: The mechanism through which each completed cycle becomes part of a higher‑order structure.

Why RKCT?

Many theories describe change, but few explain how stability and change interact across multiple levels. RKCT fills this gap by showing how recursive cycles generate increasingly complex forms of knowledge. It provides a conceptual bridge between evolutionary biology, cognitive science, cultural theory, and artificial intelligence.

Current Version

The current stable version of the model is RKCT 4.4. It integrates all previous refinements, clarifies the boundaries between phases, and formalizes recursive embedding as a central structural principle. The evolution of the model is documented on the Evolution page.
